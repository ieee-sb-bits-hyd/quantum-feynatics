Enter the Era of Quantum Utility.

QuantumVerse Vol. 3 is a unique three-day event hosted by the Quantum Feynatics Group and IBM Quantum. This immersive experience, combining a hands-on workshop with a competitive hackathon, is your gateway to the latest advancements where practical quantum applications are finally becoming a reality.

A Century of Quantum!

From abstract theory to tangible technology, from pure curiosity to powerful computation. Join us at Qiskit Fall Fest to celebrate 100 years of quantum mechanics and be part of the generation that builds its future.

Event & Hackathon Details

Event Details

  • The event will be held in a hybrid mode, with both in-person and virtual participation. The in-person sessions will be live-streamed and recorded for later reference.
  • All resources used during the workshop will be shared regularly with participants throughout the duration of the event.

Hackathon Details

  • In-person only participants will be eligible to take part in the hackathon. Virtual participants will have view-only access to the problem statement.
  • Exclusive goodies from IBM will be provided only to the hackathon winners.

Your Journey Through The Verse

  • Kickoff

    10:30 - 10:45

    A brief introduction to the workshop's agenda and a high-level overview of quantum computing and its various applications in science and industry.

  • Speaker Session

    11:00 - 12:00

    A potential speaker from IBM will guide participants into the field of quantum computing, offering perspectives on its present state, recent progress, IBM‚Äôs roadmap for developing quantum computers, and the applications where it outperforms classical approaches. This session will equip participants with a well-rounded understanding of the domain, spark their interest in the field, and provide an opportunity to have their questions addressed directly.

  • Lunch Break

    12:00 - 13:00

  • Fundamental of Quantum Computing

    13:00 - 16:00

    This session introduces the fundamental building blocks of quantum computation, covering the necessary mathematical foundations, single and multi-qubit systems, and the design of quantum circuits. Participants will also be introduced to IBM‚Äôs Quantum Computing SDK, Qiskit, which enables them to code and explore quantum circuits in detail while providing tools essential for practical quantum computing applications and beyond.

  • Working with Qiskit SDK

    10:00 - 13:00

    A comprehensive, hands-on and theory-guided session on programming with Qiskit, where participants will learn to construct, simulate, and run quantum circuits on a quantum computer. Key topics include using quantum circuit simulators, performing circuit measurements with Qiskit Primitives, circuit transpilation, and exploring both IBM‚Äôs real and simulated backends.

  • Lunch Break

    13:00 - 14:00

  • Expert Panel Discussion

    14:00 - 15:00

    A moderated discussion featuring a panel of experts in quantum computing. The discussion will explore current technical and practical challenges, emerging trends in research and industry, and potential opportunities for innovation and application. Participants will gain insights from professionals and have the chance to get their questions addressed, fostering a deeper understanding of the field and its future directions.

  • Understanding Quantum Entanglement

    15:00 - 16:30

    This session delves into quantum entanglement, a fundamental principle that sets quantum systems apart from classical ones. Participants will explore its key properties and learn about various applications that leverage entanglement, highlighting why quantum algorithms achieve capabilities beyond classical methods.

  • Introduction to Quantum Error Correction

    10:00 - 12:00

    In the current NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) era, quantum computers are highly sensitive to environmental noise, which can cause errors or decoherence. This session introduces the fundamental concepts of quantum error correction and the techniques used to protect quantum information in such noisy, intermediate-scale devices.

  • Quantum Hackathon

    13:00 - 17:00

    Test your knowledge in a challenging hackathon, applying the concepts and skills you‚Äôve gained throughout the workshop.
